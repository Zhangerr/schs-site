College Corner

 

Both the UC system, and Stanford University state that students should take as many honors courses as possible to look good for admissions, however, those courses do not necessarily need to culminate in an examination (such as an AP test).  Honors Physics fits this criterion - it is the highest level of physics available at SCHS, but does not necessarily culminate in an examination (such as the AP Physics B exam).  While students may take the AP Physics B exam, if they choose, this course is not a test-prep course for that test (it is, however, a course which will fulfill the requirements to take the IB Standard Level Physics exam and the SAT II Physics Subject exam).  There is no mandatory standardized exam at the end of the Honors Physics course (other than the final exam for the class).

 Please read the following information from the UC and Stanford websites - their point is that students should take the most challenging classes available to them at their high school  (AP, IB or Honors courses fit this description)
